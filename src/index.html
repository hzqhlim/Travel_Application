Famous Places to visit in Cancun

Isla Mujeres

The white sands are replaced by turquoise
water, warm and perfect for a swim. Don’t miss your chance to explore Cancun’s best-kept secret!

Museo Subacuático del Arte

For those who want to get up close to the sculptures themselves, then try scuba diving or snorkelling. It’ll give you a totally different perspective!

Xcaret Park

There are more than 40 attractions set
across this 250 acre park, so you’re sure to find something cool and interesting for you and your family.

Coco Bongo

A great place to visit in Cancun at night.
You’ll have your own waiter service and a lot more room to manoeuvre, while still getting to enjoy the epic shows!

La Isla Shopping Village

Whatever you want to buy, you’ll find it here. La Isla is a mixture of Mexican chain stores, souvenir shops, and some well-known global retailers.

Chichén Itzá

If you can stand being part of such a huge
crowd, the spring and autumn equinoxes are great times to visit!

Famous Places to visit in San Miguel de Alende

Parroquia de San Miguel Arcangel

This church is characterized by its pink exterior and wedding cake towers designed by indigenous stone mason Zeferino Gutierrez.

El Charco del Ingenio

While it’s labeled as a botanical garden, it is better described as a nature preserve with a large collection of cactus and other native plants.

Finca la Devoción

Among the beautiful grounds, this property includes an event space, a boutique hotel and thermal water pool, and a mezcal tasting bar featuring El Tinieblo.

La Gruta Hot Springs

On the thermal waters at La Gruta Spa include several pools and a steamy cave (gruta) of warmer water that visitors can access by swimming through a picturesque tunnel.

Templo de San Francisco

a neoclassical church that features carved stone figures and a churrigueresque facade. Built in the early 18th century, this was part of a Franciscan monastery complex.

The Sanctuary of Atotonilco

This church is regularly referred to as the “Sistine Chapel of Mexico” because of the intricate murals that adorn the interior archways, walls, and ceiling.

Famous Places to visit in Mexico City

The National Museum of Anthropology

The National Museum of Anthropology lies in Chapultepec Park and is hard to miss due to the huge monolithic figure marking its entrance.

The National Palace

Don't miss The History of Mexico mural by Diego Rivera, which adorns the grand staircase.

Templo Mayor and the Great Pyramid of Tenochtitlán

A highlight of a visit is a walkway past the precinct of the aristocratic "winged warriors," where remains of residences decorated with multi-colored reliefs have been unearthed, along with evidence of the original paintwork.

The House of Tiles

The House of Tiles is now a restaurant and an evocative venue to dine al fresco in the building's spectacular courtyard, surrounded by what is one very large work of art.

Zócalo

The beating heart of Mexico City is Zócalo - the Plaza de la Constitución (Constitution Square) - where the country's first constitution was proclaimed in 1813. Entry is free.

The Palace of Fine Arts

If you're able to see a performance here, you'll also be rewarded with a chance to enjoy the theater's stunning interior décor,.

Famous Places to visit in Puerto Vallarta

The Jungle

This lush oasis is home to miles of hiking trails, stunning waterfalls, and exotic wildlife. It’s the perfect place for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.

The Cathedral of Our Lady of Guadalupe

Even if you’re not religious, the cathedral is an awe-inspiring sight. The cathedral is open to the public every day, and Mass is held several times a week.

The Malecón

The Malecón is the iconic boardwalk that runs along Puerto Vallarta’s waterfront. Whether you’re looking for a place to relax or party, the Malecón has it all!

The Marina

The Marina is a great place to go for a walk, take in the sights, and enjoy some fresh seafood that making it the perfect place to spend an evening out on the town.

Playa de los Muertos

The popular spot for snorkeling, swimming, and surfing. One of the most fun things to do in Puerto Vallarta without a doubt!

The Zona Romántica

This lively area is home to dozens of restaurants, bars, and nightclubs, making it the perfect place to party the night away.

Famous Places to visit in Playa Del Carmen

Parque Los Fundadores

At the beach front of the park is a new bronze sculpture placed there in late 2011. It is entitled “Portal Maya 2012”. You can walk around this statue and see it up close.

Cenote Chaak Tun

This beautiful, natural environment is an underground paradise of crystal-clear water and prehistoric rock formations that look absolutely otherworldly when bathed in the beam of natural sunlight that trickles in from above.

Mamitas Beach Club

Playa del Carmen has some beautiful beaches, being right on the Caribbean Sea, so if you want to maximize your time it may be worth investing an afternoon in a beach club likes Mamitas Beach Club.

Mayan Ruins, Coba

Coba is one of the only sites remaining in
Mexico where you can still climb the temple stairways.

The Fives Rooftop

You don't have to be a guest to visit - you can simply buy a day pass. The pool day passes work similarly to the beach clubs in that they guarantee admission, a chair, and a credit for food.

Rio Secreto

A spectacular underground fantasy world that is straight out of a Sci-Fi film. Don a wetsuit and prepare yourself to be awed by one of the most impressive underground cave systems in the world.
